Our Services

Asbestos Abatement

Certified Asbestos Removal Inc., provides home-owners and businesses with certified and adequately trained individuals readily available to provide complete asbestos removal, asbestos inspections, pre- demolition reports, and bulk sampling and air sampling services for both residential and commercial properties.

Asbestos Testing

Prior to the start of any renovation or demolition project, residential or commercial properties that are suspected of containing asbestos will need to be tested to confirm the presence of asbestos within the building materials. Certified Asbestos Removal Inc., has knowledgeable and competent individuals available to properly sample your building materials suspected of containing asbestos. All sampling of materials should be representative and conducted in accordance to the methodologies described within the Alberta Asbestos Abatement Manual

Asbestos Air Sampling

Certified Asbestos Removal Inc., has knowledgeable and competent individuals capable of sampling for asbestos fibres for the purpose of complying with the Alberta Health and Safety Occupational Exposure Limit. Air samples are taken prior to the start of the work (background samples), during the abatement activities (on a daily basis), and upon completion of the job (before the enclosure is dismantled). Personal occupational samples are also collected daily to ensure that the work is being performed without the release of fibres, and to make certain that workers are wearing appropriate respirators for the type of work. All air monitoring is conducted by competent personnel while following the methods specified in the Occupational Health and Safety Legislation (NIOSH Method 7400).

What is Asbestos?

Asbestos is a hazardous building material that must be inhaled to cause disease. Intact and undisturbed asbestos presents no direct health hazard but does present a potential exposure hazard should fibres be released and inhaled.
Asbestos is the common name given to a group of naturally occurring mineral silicates that can be separated into flexible fibres. The name asbestos comes from the Greek word meaning unquenchable or indestructible. The main properties that make asbestos useful are its incombustibility, strength and flexibility when separated into fibres. It is also effective as a reinforcing or binding agent when combined with cement or plastic. Asbestos can be found in materials such as:

  • Building exteriors (e.g., cement siding panels, soffits, roof panels, mastics, stucco);
  • Flooring (e.g., vinyl asbestos tiles, sheet vinyl flooring, and floor leveling compound);
  • Ceilings (e.g., ceiling tiles, acoustic and stipple finishes, and plaster or drywall);
  • Walls (e.g., plaster or drywall joint compound, stippled finishes, and cement panels); and
  • Insulation (e.g., vermiculite, boilers, vessels, pipes, ducts, floors, ceilings and walls)

Why Test for Asbestos?

Asbestos-related diseases (e.g., Asbestosis, Lung Cancer, and Mesothelioma) are caused by asbestos fibres that are inhaled and settle in the lungs. Once embedded in lung tissue, the fibres may remain within the body for extended periods. It is important to test for the presence of asbestos before initiating any building repairs, improvements on structures or the demolition of buildings that were built prior to 1990.
